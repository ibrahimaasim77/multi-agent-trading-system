**Operational correction, more important than today's market read:** yesterday's journal (6/23) flagged a serious-sounding concern — "no evidence either routine ran," zero Gmail hits despite exhaustive search. That conclusion was **wrong**, and the bug is now identified: this post-mortem's Gmail searches (`search_threads`) exclude drafts by default, and both the morning and intraday routines deliver their output exclusively via `create_draft` (per ADR-005 — Gmail is "draft only," Calendar carries the real push). Pulling `list_drafts` directly today surfaced complete, well-reasoned writeups for 6/18, 6/19 (holiday), 6/22, 6/23, and 6/24 — every single trading day has a full audit trail. The system has never silently failed to run; the post-mortem's own audit method has been silently blind to its primary evidence source. This is now fixed for future runs (use `list_drafts`, not `search_threads`, when looking for routine output) and should be called out directly to the user since it invalidates 6/23's "operational flag" framing.

**Second, more consequential finding, also newly surfaced by reading the actual draft history (6/18 onward):** the system has now gone **4-for-4 trading days with zero trades placed** (6/18, 6/22, 6/23, 6/24 — 6/19 was a holiday). That means Priority-1 ("catalyst on CORE ticker → $100") is structurally dead — no catalyst, however perfect, can ever produce a CORE-ticker trade under current pricing. The system's only live path to a trade is the opportunistic single-name tier ($75 cap, requires price >$15, mkt cap >$5B, index membership, fresh non-rumor catalyst) — a narrow band that today's two best-performing candidates (WEN +25.7%, RUN +12.5%) both fell outside of (price floor and mkt-cap/index floor respectively).
